SCS Engineers · 2024–Present
AI Tooling

AI Sourcing Tool: National Engineering Talent Map Built with Claude Code

Problem

Licensed niche talent (PE, PG, BCEE) in solid waste, landfill design, and environmental compliance does not surface reliably through standard channels. LinkedIn returns broad results, and SeekOut coverage thins out in the smaller markets where many projects sit. External search fees for hard-to-fill technical roles routinely topped $30K per hire. The recruiting function across 70+ offices and 44 states needed a comprehensive, shared map of competitor and adjacent-industry talent that the standard stack could not produce.

Action

Designed a continuously refreshed national talent intelligence map using Claude Code, built and refined over two weeks and maintained as a living resource. It ingested direct competitors, adjacent firms including Tetra Tech, Jacobs, TRC Companies, and Republic Services, municipal and county public works entities, and SWANA and RAM/SWANA conference rosters. Output lived in a single live spreadsheet, each row carrying current and prior employers, a narrative experience summary, technical specialties mapped to internal business unit needs, multi-state licensure, LinkedIn URL, and verified contact information. Every contact was tied to a specific hiring manager and business unit, so recruiters, hiring managers, and leaders could filter by specialty and act the same day.

Result

Mapped 540+ named contacts across 181 organizations. Closed 7 licensed niche engineering hires in 6 months, avoiding an estimated $200K+ in external search fees, and displaced roughly $40K+ in planned sourcing spend by removing the need for a contract sourcer and paid subscriptions. Compressed an estimated 2 to 3 months, around 270 to 405 hours, of manual sourcing into a two-week build. Adopted across recruiters, hiring managers, and executive leadership as a shared asset for sourcing, competitive talent mapping, and workforce planning.

SCS Engineers · 2024–Present
Process

Intake Redesign: Interview-to-Offer Ratio from 5:1 to 3:1

Problem

SCS ran a highly manual recruiting process with no structured intake. Requisitions opened without alignment on compensation, credentials, interview format, or candidate profile, producing a 5:1 interview-to-offer ratio across field and technical searches.

Action

Built a structured intake process. Aligned hiring managers on compensation, credentials, interview format, and candidate profile before each requisition opened, and standardized passive outreach across iCIMS and LinkedIn Recruiter.

Result

Interview-to-offer ratio improved from 5:1 to 3:1 across field and technical searches. Fewer interviews per hire, and tighter alignment before sourcing began.

Experian · 2021–2024
Closing

Pre-Close and Negotiation: Offer Acceptance Up 18%

Problem

Senior technical and GTM offers were exposed to competing bids, and compensation talks handed back to HRBPs lost candidates late in the process. Aging searches sat in the backlog without a clear diagnosis.

Action

Personally ran multi-round technical pre-close and negotiated compensation directly with candidates against multiple competing offers, protecting equity, leveling, and counteroffer expectations through to signature. Audited every search older than 60 days and renegotiated scope where the blocker was a misaligned profile rather than a sourcing gap.

Result

Offer acceptance rates up 18%. Open requisition backlog down 12%. Sustained 60+ hires a year across technical, GTM, and corporate functions while carrying 15 to 25 active requisitions.

Prime Healthcare · 2018–2020
