📁 Case Studies

Four documented examples of real problems I identified and solved with specific actions and measurable outcomes.

SCS Engineers · 2024–Present
12 Niche Engineering Hires in 6 Months Across Hard-to-Fill Markets
Sourcing
Problem
SCS Engineers operates across 70+ offices in 44 states hiring civil, environmental, systems, and data engineering professionals in markets where traditional job boards have almost no reach. Candidates with niche engineering licenses and certifications in rural or secondary markets are not actively applying. Agency fees were running high with limited results.
Action
Rebuilt the sourcing approach around passive outreach. Introduced SeekOut as the primary sourcing tool, filtering by engineering specialty and geographic market. Built Boolean search strings targeting candidates by license type, certification, and proximity to project sites. Established a consistent 48-hour hiring manager feedback cadence so pipeline never stalled. Used LinkedIn Recruiter for secondary outreach on roles where SeekOut coverage was thinner.
Result
12 niche engineering hires closed in the first 6 months through direct passive sourcing. Agency reliance reduced significantly, saving over $200K in fees. Passive outreach response rates improved 16% through more targeted Boolean search strategies.
SCS Engineers · 2024–Present
Intake Process Redesign: Interview-to-Offer Ratio from 5:1 to 3:1
Process
Problem
No structured intake process. Hiring managers submitted reqs without clear requirements, compensation alignment, or interview frameworks. Result was a 5:1 interview-to-offer ratio meaning five candidates going through the full process for every single hire. Expensive in time, hiring manager bandwidth, and candidate experience across a 70-office national firm.
Action
Redesigned the intake protocol end-to-end. Implemented mandatory kickoff calls before any req went live. Built standardized job brief templates capturing requirements, compensation range, non-negotiables, and interview format. Established 48-hour feedback SLAs with hiring managers. Introduced SeekOut for passive sourcing in markets where job boards weren't reaching qualified candidates.
Result
Interview-to-offer ratio came down from 5:1 to 3:1 for field and technical roles within 6 months. Fewer interviews, better candidates, faster fills.
Experian · 2021–2024
18% Offer Acceptance Improvement Through Pre-Close Process Ownership
Offer Strategy
Problem
Offer declines were happening too often at the final stage on technical roles where candidates had multiple competing offers. The gap was almost always compensation misalignment that surfaced too late in the process rather than being addressed upfront.
Action
Took personal ownership of the pre-close process on every search. Had explicit compensation conversations with candidates earlier, before the final interview rather than after. Surfaced competing offers and total comp expectations upfront, then used that information to align hiring managers and Finance on a competitive offer before it was extended. Negotiated directly with candidates on the final offer.
Result
Offer acceptance rates improved 18% through earlier and more direct compensation alignment. Fewer last-minute declines and less time lost re-opening searches.
Prime Healthcare · 2019–2020
5 Executive Placements Through Direct Passive Sourcing with Zero Search Firm Fees
Executive Search
Problem
Prime Healthcare was relying on external search firms for executive and C-suite placements across a national hospital network operating 45+ facilities in 14 states. Search firm fees on executive placements are significant and the quality of candidates was not consistently better than what direct sourcing could produce.
Action
Built direct sourcing strategies for executive roles using LinkedIn Recruiter passive outreach, targeted boolean search, and network referrals from within the healthcare system. Took personal ownership of the full executive search process from initial outreach through close, managing candidate relationships directly without a search firm intermediary.
Result
Five executive-level placements closed through direct passive sourcing, eliminating external search firm fees entirely on those searches. Candidates sourced and closed internally at a fraction of the cost with faster timelines than the search firm model.
